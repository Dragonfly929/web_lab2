About Vincent van Gogh

Vincent Willem van Gogh (30 March 1853 – 29 July 1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter who is among the most famous and influential figures in the history of Western art. In just over a decade, he created about 2,100 artworks, including around 860 oil paintings, most of which date from the last two years of his life. They include landscapes, still lifes, portraits, and self-portraits, and are characterized by bold colors and dramatic, impulsive brushwork that contributed to the foundations of modern art.

Van Gogh struggled with mental health issues throughout his life, and his fame grew only after his death. He experienced periods of depression and fits of mental instability, which led to his eventual suicide at the age of 37. Despite his struggles, van Gogh's artistic legacy lives on, and his works continue to inspire and captivate audiences around the world.

Author's Works

The Starry Night

The Starry Night (Dutch: De sterrennacht) is an oil-on-canvas painting by the Dutch Post-Impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh. Painted in June 1889, it depicts the view from the east-facing window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, just before sunrise, with the addition of an imaginary village. It has been in the permanent collection of the Museum of Modern Art in New York City since 1941, acquired through the Lillie P. Bliss Bequest. Widely regarded as Van Gogh's magnum opus, The Starry Night is one of the most recognizable paintings in Western art.

Almond Blossom

Almond Blossom is an oil-on-canvas painting by the Dutch Post-Impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh. Painted in February 1890, it depicts branches of almond trees in blossom against a blue sky. The painting was a gift to his brother Theo and sister-in-law Jo, who had just given birth to a son, Vincent Willem, named after his uncle. Almond Blossom is one of van Gogh's best-known works and is celebrated for its vibrant colors and expressive brushwork.

The Sower

The Sower is an oil-on-canvas painting by the Dutch Post-Impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh. Painted in 1888, it depicts a peasant sowing seeds in a field. The painting is part of a series of works van Gogh created on the theme of sowers, inspired by the rural life he observed during his time in Arles, France. The Sower reflects van Gogh's interest in depicting the dignity and toil of working-class individuals, as well as his fascination with the cycle of life and growth.
